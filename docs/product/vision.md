# Vision

## Problem

People in Kuwait who want mental health support often face friction before they ever reach a session:

- it is not always easy to discover suitable therapists digitally
- booking and payment can feel fragmented or unclear
- privacy concerns make users cautious about sharing personal information
- remote session experiences can feel unreliable or unprofessional

Mindscape exists to reduce that friction and make seeking care feel calm, private, and trustworthy.

## Target Users

### Primary users

- adults in Kuwait looking for therapy or mental health support
- users who prefer booking and attending remotely from their phone
- users who care about discretion, clear pricing, and simple scheduling

### Secondary users

- licensed therapists whose profiles and availability appear in the system
- small operations or admin staff managing provider data and booking issues

## Value Proposition

Mindscape offers a localized, mobile-first mental health experience for Kuwait where the path from intent to session is simple:

1. discover a therapist
2. choose a suitable time
3. pay through a locally familiar flow
4. attend the session inside the app

The product should feel less like navigating a healthcare system and more like entering a respectful, well-run digital clinic.

## Kuwait Context

- payment flow should support methods familiar in Kuwait through MyFatoorah
- trust signals matter more than aggressive growth patterns in this category
- users may expect Arabic support eventually even if MVP launches English-first
- appointment clarity, therapist legitimacy, and privacy language are especially important in a mental health product

## Trust And Privacy Position

Mindscape should treat privacy as part of the product, not only a compliance task.

- sensitive information is collected only when needed
- therapist discovery should feel professional and transparent
- booking, payment, and session status should always be explicit
- the UI tone should remain calm and non-judgmental
- access to session and payment data must be tightly scoped

## Product Promise

Mindscape helps users in Kuwait access therapy with less uncertainty and less friction, while preserving dignity, privacy, and clear communication throughout the experience.

## Design Direction

The app should feel:

- calm
- credible
- modern
- emotionally safe
- operationally reliable

## What Success Looks Like

The MVP is successful if a first-time user can do the following without assistance:

1. sign up or sign in
2. browse therapists with enough information to choose
3. book an available time slot
4. complete payment successfully
5. join the session from the app at the right time
6. review the outcome later in booking history

## Explicit Non-Goals

- replacing emergency mental health services
- building a social community product
- building an AI therapist or chatbot in MVP
- solving every clinic operation in version one

## Assumptions

- Assumption: MVP launches English-first but must remain structurally ready for Arabic.
- Assumption: therapist profiles are curated by operations rather than self-serve in the first release.
- Assumption: trust and execution quality matter more than broad feature breadth at launch.
